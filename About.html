Bulldog Bytes

About

Overview

Bulldog Bytes is an interdisciplinary collaboration at MSU to introduce computing and cybersecurity to youth, with a focus on K12 in the state of Mississippi.The Bulldog Bytes summer camp program was created in 2013 with a grant from NCWIT AspireIT under the leadership of CSE student Rian Walker. With NCWIT funding in 2014, women faculty and students from Mississippi State University Art, Computer Science and Engineering, and English Departments (ACE) collaborated to offer residential computing camps for girls that integrate programming with writing, rhetoric, and visual design. In 2015, AspireIT again funded the girls camps along with GenCyber funding, and boys camps were also offered through GenCyber.

Initially starting off as an all girls camp, Bulldog Bytes Camp (since 2015) is now for boys and girls grades 6 through 12.
